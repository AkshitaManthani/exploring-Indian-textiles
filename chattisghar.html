Textiles of Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh is famous for its exquisite handwoven textiles, including the luxurious "Kosa Silk" and the traditional "Pata Weaving". These textiles showcase the state's rich craftsmanship and heritage.

Kosa Silk

Kosa silk, often referred to as Chhattisgarh silk, is known for its smooth texture and lustrous finish. The silk is made from the cocoons of the wild silkworm found in the forests of Chhattisgarh.

Weaving Technique: Kosa silk is traditionally handwoven using pit looms, often combined with cotton threads to create a smooth yet strong fabric.

Craftsmanship: The weaving of Kosa silk is an intricate process that requires high skill and patience. It is often dyed with natural colors to preserve its traditional appeal.

GI Tag: Kosa Silk (Registered in 2008, GI Number: 103)

Pata Weaving

Pata weaving is an ancient craft of handloom weaving from Chhattisgarh, which is known for its vibrant designs and beautiful patterns. The textiles are woven using traditional methods that have been passed down through generations.

Weaving Technique: Pata weaving uses natural yarns and vibrant dyes, with designs that often reflect the cultural heritage and natural surroundings of the region.

Craftsmanship: Each piece is handcrafted by skilled artisans, who meticulously create intricate patterns and designs on the loom.

GI Tag: Pata Weaving (Registered in 2011, GI Number: 141)
