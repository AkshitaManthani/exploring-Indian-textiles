Textiles of Assam

Assam, known for its rich cultural heritage, is renowned for its luxurious silks, including the unique Muga Silk and the eco-friendly Eri Silk, both of which are integral to the region’s identity and craftsmanship.

Muga Silk

Muga Silk is a rare and natural golden silk produced exclusively in Assam. It is highly prized for its rich texture, natural golden hue, and durability, making it a symbol of Assamese culture and tradition.

Technique: Muga silk is woven with a traditional handloom process, with its unique golden color being natural to the silk.

Usage: Muga silk is primarily used in making elegant sarees, scarves, and other fine textiles, often worn during festivals and special occasions.

GI Tag: Muga Silk (Registered in 2007, GI Number: 91)

Eri Silk

Eri Silk, also known as the "peace silk," is an eco-friendly, durable fabric that is produced without killing the silkworms. It is valued for its softness, comfort, and natural appeal.

Technique: Eri silk is produced through a non-violent method where the silkworms are allowed to complete their life cycle before harvesting the silk.

Usage: Eri silk is used for creating garments, shawls, and home decor items. It is preferred for its breathable quality and comfort.

GI Tag: Eri Silk (Registered in 2010, GI Number: 178)
