Textiles of Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh is renowned for its distinctive and vibrant textile heritage. Chamba Rumal and Kullu Shawls reflect the rich traditions and skilled craftsmanship of the Himalayan communities.

Chamba Rumal

Chamba Rumal is a unique embroidered textile from the Chamba region. These handkerchief-sized fabrics feature fine double satin stitch embroidery depicting religious and mythological scenes.

Technique: Needle embroidery using untwisted silk threads in both horizontal and vertical directions for a double-sided design.

Significance: Traditionally gifted during weddings and auspicious events.

GI Tag: Chamba Rumal (Registered in 2007, GI Number: 85)

Kullu Shawls

Kullu Shawls are vibrant woolen textiles known for their geometric patterns and bright colors. Handwoven by local artisans, these shawls are a symbol of Himachali identity and elegance.

Material: Made using sheep wool, angora, or pashmina wool depending on the quality.

Design: Bold motifs like diamonds, triangles, and stripes in red, black, and white colors.

GI Tag: Kullu Shawl (Registered in 2005, GI Number: 5)
