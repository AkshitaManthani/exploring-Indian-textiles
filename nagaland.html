Textiles of Nagaland

Nagaland, known for its rich cultural heritage, is home to exquisite handwoven textiles such as Loin Loom and the vibrant Naga Shawls, both of which are integral to the indigenous Naga tribes.

Loin Loom

Loin Loom weaving is a traditional weaving technique practiced by the Naga tribes, where the weaver ties a loom around their waist while working. This technique produces highly intricate designs and is used for creating shawls, skirts, and other garments.

Technique: The weaver ties the loom around their waist, using the tension of their body to weave the fabric with precision and care.

Craftsmanship: Loin Loom textiles feature unique designs that represent the Naga tribes’ cultural symbols, often involving geometric patterns and natural dyes.

Naga Shawls

Naga Shawls are iconic garments crafted by the indigenous Naga tribes. These shawls are known for their bold, vibrant colors and intricate handwoven patterns, often signifying the wearer’s tribe or social status.

Technique: Naga shawls are traditionally handwoven using cotton or wool, with designs that hold cultural significance.

Symbolism: The patterns and colors of Naga shawls often signify the wearer's tribe, social rank, and accomplishments.
