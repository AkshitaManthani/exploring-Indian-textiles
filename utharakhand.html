Textiles of Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand’s traditional textiles reflect its mountainous culture and deep-rooted customs, with Thulma providing warmth and Rangwali Pichhora embodying festive elegance.

Thulma

Thulma is a thick woolen textile traditionally woven in Uttarakhand, mainly used as blankets and wraps. These are made using coarse wool to endure the cold Himalayan climate.

Material & Weave: Made from sheep wool and woven on handlooms in a double-layered weave.

Use: Used as quilts, floor mats, and blankets in colder regions of the state.

Crafting Areas: Primarily made in Almora, Pithoragarh, and Chamoli districts.

Rangwali Pichhora

The Rangwali Pichhora is a traditional garment worn by Kumaoni women during auspicious occasions. It features bold red-orange colors with religious and symbolic motifs.

Designs: Floral, swastika, and sun motifs created using block printing and embroidery.

Material: Cotton or synthetic fabric, decorated with bright colored patterns and dots.

Cultural Role: Essential in weddings, Navratri, and family rituals; symbolizes marital bliss and cultural pride.
