Textiles of Telangana

Telangana is renowned for its handloom heritage, especially the lustrous Gadwal Silks and the geometric precision of Pochampally Ikat, both of which reflect the cultural and artistic traditions of the state.

Gadwal Silk

Gadwal silk originates from the town of Gadwal in Telangana. Known for its lightweight fabric, contrasting zari borders, and cotton-silk blend, it’s famous for its seamless fusion of strength and elegance.

Weaving Technique: Interlocking weaving (kuttu technique) of cotton body with silk pallu and zari borders.

Craftsmanship: Traditionally woven by hand, each saree reflects skilled craftsmanship and takes up to a week or more to complete.

GI Tag: Gadwal Sarees (Registered in 2010, GI Number: 187)

Pochampally Ikat

Pochampally Ikat hails from the Bhoodan Pochampally region. It features intricate patterns created through a pre-dyeing process, where yarns are tie-dyed before weaving. The result is a symmetrical, geometric textile marvel.

Weaving Technique: Ikat (resist dyeing) on warp and/or weft threads before weaving. Both single and double ikat are practiced.

Artisan Work: Involves precision planning and skilled dyeing; often done by entire families.

GI Tag: Pochampally Ikat (Registered in 2005, GI Number: 4)
