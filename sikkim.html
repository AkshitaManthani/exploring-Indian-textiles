Textiles of Sikkim

Sikkim’s textile tradition is deeply rooted in the indigenous Lepcha community, whose unique weaving art reflects nature, heritage, and cultural beliefs.

Lepcha Weave

The Lepcha weave is traditionally handwoven using a back-strap loom. These textiles feature vibrant stripes and symbolic motifs that represent mountains, rivers, and harmony with nature.

Technique: Handwoven on traditional looms, often by women, using cotton or wool threads.

Design: Rich in geometric and symbolic patterns passed down through generations.

Usage: Commonly used in traditional Lepcha attire like Dumdyam and Thara, symbolizing cultural identity and pride.
