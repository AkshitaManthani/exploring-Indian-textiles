Textiles of Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh is renowned for its rich textile traditions, particularly the artistic **Bagh Printing** and elegant **Maheshwari Silks**, reflecting centuries of skilled craftsmanship.

Bagh Printing

Bagh Printing is a traditional block printing technique using natural colors, originating from the town of Bagh. It is known for its intricate motifs and bold colors, especially red and black.

Technique: Wooden hand blocks are used to print geometric and floral patterns onto cotton and silk fabrics. Natural dyes derived from plants and minerals are used.

Artisan Work: Involves washing, dyeing, printing, and multiple rounds of treatment to ensure deep color fixation.

GI Tag: Bagh Prints of Madhya Pradesh (Registered in 2008, GI Number: 108)

Maheshwari Silks

Maheshwari silk sarees originate from the town of Maheshwar. Known for their light weight and graceful appearance, they are distinguished by their checks, stripes, and floral borders.

Weaving Technique: Handwoven with silk and cotton blend yarns, often with zari work on the borders and pallu.

Craftsmanship: The weavers use pit looms to craft fine and elegant sarees, traditionally made by women artisans.

GI Tag: Maheshwari Sarees & Fabrics (Registered in 2010, GI Number: 182)
