Textiles of Gujarat

Gujarat holds a prestigious place in India’s textile heritage with its luxurious Patola sarees and intricate Ajrakh block prints, known for their vibrant patterns and deep-rooted cultural significance.

Patola Sarees

Patola sarees are a form of double ikat silk textiles woven in Patan. These sarees are famed for their vibrant colors, geometric patterns, and precision, making them highly valued heirlooms.

Weaving Technique: Double ikat, where both warp and weft threads are resist-dyed before weaving.

Artisan Work: Extremely time-intensive, with a single saree taking 6 months to a year to produce by skilled weavers.

GI Tag: Patan Patola (Registered in 2013, GI Number: 217)

Ajrakh Printing

Ajrakh is a centuries-old block printing technique originating from Kutch. Known for its deep indigo and red tones, Ajrakh prints are made using natural dyes and complex, symmetrical patterns.

Technique: Resist printing using natural dyes, with multiple block layers and drying steps between each color application.

Craftsmanship: Involves detailed planning and alignment of blocks, and is mostly practiced by Khatri communities.

GI Tag: Ajrakh Printing of Kutch (Registered in 2020, GI Number: 344)
