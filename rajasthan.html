Textiles of Rajasthan

Rajasthan's textile legacy bursts with color and craftsmanship, particularly through the vibrant tie-dye Bandhani technique and the delicate elegance of Kota Doria fabric.

Bandhani

Bandhani, a traditional tie-dye technique, involves plucking the cloth with fingernails into many tiny bindings that form a figurative design. It is practiced extensively in Jaipur, Udaipur, and Jodhpur.

Technique: Fabric is tied in intricate patterns and dyed in vibrant colors. The tied areas resist the dye, resulting in beautiful dotted designs.

Artisan Work: A laborious handcraft done mainly by women; requires precision in both tying and dyeing.

GI Tag: Rajasthan Bandhani (Registered in 2010, GI Number: 207)

Kota Doria

Kota Doria is a lightweight fabric made of cotton and silk in a square check pattern, locally known as 'khat'. It is woven in the town of Kota and nearby regions.

Weaving Technique: Woven on traditional pit looms using a blend of cotton and silk with fine square checks.

Craftsmanship: Delicate and airy, requiring skilled artisans to maintain the perfect 'khat' design.

GI Tag: Kota Doria (Registered in 2005, GI Number: 8)
