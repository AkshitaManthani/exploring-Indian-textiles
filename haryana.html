Textiles of Haryana

Haryana is known for its Punja Durries, handwoven floor coverings that showcase bold geometric patterns and vibrant colors, blending utility with artistic tradition.

Punja Durries

Punja Durries are flat-woven rugs made using a hand-held tool called the 'punja'. These are widely used in households and exported for their durability and charm.

Weaving Technique: Interlocking weft threads using a punja comb on a vertical or horizontal loom. Made with cotton or jute for strength and versatility.

Design: Features bold stripes, checks, and traditional motifs in bright colors. Often reversible and lightweight.

Craftsmanship: Woven mostly by women artisans, these durries are a symbol of self-reliance and rural craft heritage in Haryana.

Recognition: Punja Durries are registered with a GI Tag (GI Number: 161, Registered in 2009).
