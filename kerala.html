Textiles of Kerala

Kerala is known for its serene backwaters, but its textile tradition, featuring the renowned Kasavu Mundun and Mundum Neriyathum, reflects the state’s rich cultural heritage.

Kasavu Mundun

Kasavu Mundun is a traditional Kerala garment, often worn during festivals. It’s made of fine cotton with golden borders, symbolizing the rich cultural history of the region.

Weaving Technique: Woven using cotton threads with gold zari borders.

Craftsmanship: The traditional technique involves hand-weaving to ensure smooth texture and sheen in the fabric.

GI Tag: Kasavu Mundun (Registered in 2005, GI Number: 121)

Mundum Neriyathum

Mundum Neriyathum is a traditional Kerala outfit consisting of a mundu (lower garment) and neriyathu (upper garment), usually in white with golden or colorful borders, often worn during important cultural occasions.

Weaving Technique: Handwoven cotton fabric with zari or colored thread borders, showcasing traditional artistry.

Craftsmanship: The garment requires a skilled handloom weaving process that emphasizes smoothness and fine detail in design.

GI Tag: Mundum Neriyathum (Registered in 2007, GI Number: 78)
