Textiles of Meghalaya

Meghalaya’s weaving tradition is rich, with "Eri Silk" and "Dakmanda" reflecting the state's cultural identity and the skill of its artisans.

Eri Silk

Eri Silk, also known as the “Ahimsa Silk,” is produced in Meghalaya through a process that ensures no harm is done to the silkworm. This silk is known for its softness, durability, and warmth.

Technique: The Eri silk is produced using hand-spun yarn from the Eri silkworm. The fabric is then handwoven using traditional techniques.

Significance: This silk is highly valued for its eco-friendly production and is commonly used for making garments like shawls and scarves.

Dakmanda

Dakmanda is a traditional textile from Meghalaya, woven by the local artisans using techniques passed down through generations. Known for its intricate designs and durable nature, it is used in making various traditional garments.

Technique: Dakmanda is woven using a combination of locally sourced fibers and handloom techniques to create geometric and floral patterns.

Craftsmanship: The production of Dakmanda requires high skill and attention to detail, often involving complex patterns and slow weaving techniques.
