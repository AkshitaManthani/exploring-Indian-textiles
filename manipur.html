Textiles of Manipur

Manipur's textiles are a reflection of its vibrant culture, with Saphee Lanphee and Moirang Phee standing out as traditional handwoven treasures. These textiles are not just pieces of fabric, but also representations of the state's artistry and heritage.

Saphee Lanphee

Saphee Lanphee is a unique textile from Manipur, often created by the women of the state. It is woven using a traditional technique that results in a light, soft fabric with intricate designs.

Technique: The fabric is woven using a traditional handloom, where the weaver creates delicate patterns and designs, often showcasing cultural symbols.

Craftsmanship: Known for its fine texture and artistry, Saphee Lanphee is often worn for special occasions and holds great cultural significance.

Moirang Phee

Moirang Phee is another traditional textile of Manipur, known for its vibrant patterns and cultural richness. The weaving process involves a blend of skill and artistry, producing garments of high aesthetic value.

Technique: The fabric is woven using a traditional loom, with designs reflecting cultural themes and folklore.

Significance: Moirang Phee is commonly used in ceremonial attire, representing the state's deep-rooted cultural heritage.
