Sambalpuri Ikat, a traditional weaving technique from Odisha, India, utilizes a tie-dye process (known as "Bandha") to create intricate patterns on the warp and weft threads before weaving. The craft has a history dating back to the 12th century, when it was introduced by the Bhulia community. The process involves tying, dyeing, and weaving, resulting in vibrant, double-sided designs.

Bomkal

Bomkal is a type of fabric with a distinct weave, known for its intricate designs and durable quality. Its history is rooted in Indian weaving traditions, and the weaving methods are primarily done on handlooms, utilizing techniques passed down through generations

Dongria Kondh

Dongria Kondh textiles, particularly their vibrant shawls called "Kapdaganda," have a rich history rooted in their cultural and spiritual beliefs. The weaving process involves a unique combination of hand-weaving and intricate embroidery, with each shawl telling a story of the Dongria Kondh community's relationship with their sacred Niyamgiri hills and their deity, Niyamraja
