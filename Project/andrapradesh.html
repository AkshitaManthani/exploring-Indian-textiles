Kalankari

Kalamkari, meaning "penwork," is an ancient Indian art form with roots dating back over 3,000 years. It's a hand-painted textile art style primarily from Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, known for intricate designs depicting scenes from mythology, nature, and daily life. The art form is associated with temple traditions and was used to create narrative depictions on cloth.

Venkatagiri silk

Venkatagiri sarees, known for their fine weaving and intricate Jamdani designs, have a history dating back to the 1700s in Andhra Pradesh, India. Initially, the weaving was patronized by the Velugoti Dynasty, and the sarees were primarily made for the royal families. The weaving process involves using traditional pit looms and the Jamdani technique, which incorporates zari (gold thread) and cotton threads to create intricate designs

Darmavaram silk

Dharmavaram, a town in Andhra Pradesh, India, is renowned for its handloom silk sarees and pavadas (long skirts). The town's history of weaving dates back to the 19th century, with silk weaving becoming a primary industry due to the abundance of mulberry trees in the area. Weaving in Dharmavaram is typically done using pit looms and frame looms fitted with jacquards, incorporating intricate designs often inspired by the Lepakshi temple
