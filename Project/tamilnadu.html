Kanchipuram Silk

Kanchipuram silks, particularly known for their sarees, have a rich history and distinctive weaving methods. Originating in the town of Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, these sarees are renowned for their luxurious appearance and intricate designs, woven by skilled artisans using the "Korvai" technique and other traditional methods. The tradition of weaving Kanchipuram silks can be traced back centuries, with the Pallava and Chola dynasties playing a significant role in its development and patronage

chettinad Cotton

Chettinad cotton sarees, particularly the Kandangi, have a rich history and unique weaving methods. Originally woven in the Chettinad region for the Chettiar community, they were known for their durability and were designed to suit the hot climate. The Kandangi weave is characterized by thick, coarse cotton with a distinctive checkered or striped pattern and contrasting borders. Weaving is done on pit looms using a flying shuttle technique, with three shuttles used to create solid, contrasting borders, according to Isha Sadhguru.

Sungudi

Sungudi sarees, known for their vibrant, dotted patterns, are a traditional handloom fabric from Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India. They are created using a tie-and-dye technique, where small wooden blocks create patterns on the fabric before dyeing, resulting in intricate dotted designs. The Saurashtrians, who migrated to South India around the 8th to 11th century and settled in Madurai, introduced this technique.
