Gadwal Silk

Gadwal silk sarees, originating in the Gadwal region of Telangana, India, have a history spanning several centuries. They are known for their unique weaving technique, "Kupadam," which combines cotton and silk. This technique involves weaving the saree's body with cotton and the borders with silk, creating a lightweight and elegant drape

Pochampally silk

Pochampally silk, known for its vibrant colors and geometric patterns, is a traditional Indian saree weaving style that originated in the Pochampally village of Telangana, India. The technique, called Ikat, involves tying and dyeing the threads before weaving, resulting in unique, blurred patterns. The art of Ikat, including Pochampally's version, has a rich history, tracing back to ancient civilizations and finding its way to India through the Silk Route

Nara silk

Nara silk, particularly during the Nara period (710-794 AD), was a key part of Japan's cultural and economic development, influenced by Chinese weaving techniques and the Silk Road. Weaving methods involved both plain and patterned weaves, including patterned twill, and gauze fabrics, with decorative techniques like resist dyeing and embroidery
