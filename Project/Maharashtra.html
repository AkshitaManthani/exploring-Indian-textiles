Paithani

Paithani sarees, originating in Paithan, Maharashtra, have a history dating back to the 2nd century BC, and were initially made with gold and silk, according to Wikipedia and Taneira. They were highly prized and used for royal attire. The unique weaving technique involves using silk and gold or silver threads, often incorporating the "extra weft" method for intricate designs

Himroo

Himroo, a luxurious fabric, has roots in Persian weaving traditions and was developed in Aurangabad, India, during the 14th century. It's characterized by intricate designs and a combination of silk and cotton. The weaving process involves using a throw-shuttle loom, with cotton for the warp and silk for the weft. The designs are typically geometric and floral, often inspired by Persian styles like Sia Jangla

Narayan Peth

Narayanpeth sarees have a history dating back to the 17th century, originating in the region under the rule of the Maratha King, Chattrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The weaving technique involves using a fly shuttle pit loom with a dobby and a fly shuttle. The sarees are typically woven with fine count cotton, often featuring geometrical designs and zari/art silk in the borders.
