Textiles of Karnataka

Karnataka's textile heritage is renowned for its elegant Mysore silk and intricate Ilkal sarees, which showcase the state's exquisite weaving traditions.

Ilkal Sarees

Ilkal sarees are famous for their unique patterns and use of a rich cotton-silk blend. Originating from the town of Ilkal in Karnataka, these sarees are distinguished by their distinctive red borders, which are woven with intricate geometric patterns.

Weaving Technique: Interlocking of cotton and silk, with traditional handloom weaving methods.

Craftsmanship: Skilled weavers use a combination of shuttle and pit looms to create these vibrant and bold designs.

GI Tag: Ilkal Sarees (Registered in 2005, GI Number: 116)

Mysore Silk

Mysore silk is a luxury textile, characterized by its smooth texture and lustrous finish. Known for its vibrant colors and intricate zari work, Mysore silk is woven in the city of Mysore, Karnataka.

Weaving Technique: Woven from pure silk threads, often with intricate zari borders and motifs.

Craftsmanship: Each saree is handwoven, taking days to weeks, depending on the design complexity.

GI Tag: Mysore Silk Sarees (Registered in 2005, GI Number: 85)
