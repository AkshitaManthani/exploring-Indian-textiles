Traditional Textiles of Uttar Pradesh

Uttar Pradesh is home to some of India’s most exquisite and heritage-rich textiles, including the opulent Banarasi silk and the intricate Chikankari embroidery of Lucknow. These crafts showcase the state's deep-rooted artistry and legacy.

Banarasi Textiles

Banarasi textiles originate from Varanasi and date back to the Mughal era. Known for their luxurious silk fabric and intricate brocade work, they are woven with gold and silver zari threads, often incorporating Mughal-inspired motifs like floral patterns and jali work.

Weaving Technique: Handwoven using Jacquard looms; incorporates real zari threads on a silk base.

Craftsmanship: Weaving a single saree can take from 15 days to 6 months, depending on complexity.

GI Tag: Banaras Brocades and Sarees (Registered in 2009, GI Number: 126)

Chikankari Embroidery

Chikankari is a form of delicate hand embroidery that flourished in Lucknow, introduced by Empress Noor Jahan during the Mughal era. It is a labor-intensive craft involving fine needlework on fabrics like muslin, cotton, or georgette.

Embroidery Method: Designs are block-printed and then hand-embroidered using stitches like bakhiya (shadow work), phanda, and keel kangan.

Artisan Work: Entirely handcrafted, requiring patience, skill, and precision.

GI Tag: Lucknow Chikan Craft (Registered in 2008, GI Number: 107)
