Textiles of West Bengal

West Bengal’s weaving legacy includes the elaborate Baluchari sarees known for mythological storytelling and the ethereal finesse of Jamdani, blending Mughal opulence with Bengali artistry.

Baluchari Saree

Baluchari sarees are characterized by intricate depictions of mythological scenes woven into the pallu and border. These artistic textiles originate from the town of Bishnupur.

Weaving Technique: Jacquard looms are used to weave elaborate motifs using silk threads and zari.

Craftsmanship: Highly skilled artisans take up to a week or more to complete one saree with detailed narrative borders.

GI Tag: Baluchari Sarees (Registered in 2009, GI Number: 121)

Jamdani Weave

Jamdani is a fine muslin textile with complex motifs handwoven directly onto the loom. It originated in Dhaka and flourishes in Bengal’s Shantipur and Kalna regions.

Technique: Supplementary weft weaving technique on fine cotton; floral and geometric motifs are inserted by hand.

Artisan Work: Labor-intensive, often taking months for a single saree; regarded as poetic artistry in fabric form.

GI Tag: Shantipur Jamdani (Registered in 2013, GI Number: 260)
