Textiles of Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh’s textile tradition is rich in the art of weaving, especially among the indigenous tribes, including the Galo and Mishmi, whose craftsmanship reflects their deep cultural ties to nature and their heritage.

Galo Weaving

The Galo tribe is known for its exquisite weaving techniques, producing vibrant, patterned textiles that are an essential part of their traditional attire.

Technique: The Galo people weave using traditional looms, creating intricate patterns on cotton or silk fabrics.

Design: Designs often reflect nature, animals, and daily life, combining geometric patterns with bold colors.

Usage: The Galo textiles are often used in making shawls, skirts, and other garments for ceremonial purposes.

Mishmi Weaving

The Mishmi people, residing in the northeastern parts of Arunachal Pradesh, are skilled in creating textiles with unique, finely woven patterns that depict their cultural traditions and beliefs.

Technique: Mishmi weaving involves fine handwoven textiles, often using cotton and wool, with detailed geometric and symbolic motifs.

Design: Their designs are often symbolic of local traditions, with intricate details, depicting animals, nature, and other cultural motifs.

Usage: Mishmi textiles are used in the creation of both everyday clothing and ritual garments, serving as a reflection of their cultural identity.
