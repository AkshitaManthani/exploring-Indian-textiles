Textiles of Mizoram

Mizoram's textile heritage is rich in colorful patterns and cultural significance, with Puanchei and Pawndum standing out as the state’s most celebrated traditional textiles.

Puanchei

Puanchei is a traditional woven textile that is vibrant and rich in color. It is primarily worn by Mizo women during festivals and ceremonial occasions.

Technique: The fabric is woven using a traditional handloom, with intricate patterns and bright colors. It is typically made from cotton or silk threads.

Significance: Puanchei is an integral part of Mizo culture, representing the beauty and cultural richness of the region. It is often worn during the Mizo New Year and other traditional events.

Pawndum

Pawndum is another unique and highly prized textile of Mizoram, woven with traditional methods that result in a distinctively beautiful fabric.

Technique: The weaving of Pawndum uses intricate techniques to create geometric patterns, with a strong emphasis on design and color contrast.

Craftsmanship: Pawndum is woven by skilled artisans, with each piece requiring a lot of effort and patience to create the detailed patterns and textures.
