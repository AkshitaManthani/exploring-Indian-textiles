Textiles of Odisha

Odisha is renowned for its vibrant textile traditions, particularly Bomkai and Sambalpuri Ikat. These textiles, known for their intricate patterns and rich cultural significance, are a testament to the state's artistic heritage.

Bomkai Textiles

Bomkai, also known as Sonepuri, is a traditional fabric from the Ganjam district. It is known for its geometric designs and unique motifs, often inspired by nature and religious themes. Bomkai textiles are typically used for sarees, stoles, and other garments.

Weaving Technique: Bomkai weaving involves intricate designs that are handwoven using traditional pit looms. The textiles are often adorned with motifs such as flowers, birds, and paisleys.

Craftsmanship: Bomkai textiles are handwoven, and the process can take days to weeks depending on the intricacy of the design. Skilled artisans combine cotton and silk threads to create these works of art.

GI Tag: Bomkai Textiles (Registered in 2010, GI Number: 164)

Sambalpuri Ikat

Sambalpuri Ikat is a famous traditional weaving technique from Odisha, known for its distinctive designs. The dyeing technique involves a process called "Ikat," where the yarns are dyed before weaving, resulting in stunning patterns and color combinations.

Weaving Technique: In Sambalpuri Ikat, the threads are pre-dyed before weaving, creating intricate and colorful designs. The technique involves both warp and weft yarns being tie-dyed.

Craftsmanship: Skilled weavers from the Sambalpur region create these textiles by hand, using traditional methods that require precision and attention to detail.

GI Tag: Sambalpuri Ikat (Registered in 2006, GI Number: 93)
