Textiles of Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh boasts a rich tradition of textile craftsmanship, including the elaborate Kalamkari art and the delicate weaves of Vekatagiri, both of which highlight the state's artistic vibrancy and heritage.

Kalamkari Textiles

Kalamkari is a traditional art form where natural dyes are used to create intricate patterns, often depicting mythological themes. It’s predominantly practiced in the towns of Machilipatnam and Srikalahasti.

Technique: Hand-painted using a pen (kalam) with natural dyes on fabrics like cotton or silk. The designs often include religious motifs and folklore.

Artisan Work: Requires detailed work and mastery in drawing with a pen, involving multiple stages of dyeing and painting.

GI Tag: Andhra Pradesh Kalamkari (Registered in 2007, GI Number: 79)

Vekatagiri Textiles

Vekatagiri textiles are known for their fine silk sarees that feature intricate weaving techniques. These silk sarees are renowned for their elegant designs and luxurious feel.

Weaving Technique: Woven using the traditional handloom technique, with rich zari work and beautiful floral patterns.

Craftsmanship: The weaving process is a labor-intensive task requiring skilled artisans to create intricate designs.

GI Tag: Vekatagiri Silk Sarees (Registered in 2005, GI Number: 6)
