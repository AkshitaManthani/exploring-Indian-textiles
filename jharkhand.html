Textiles of Jharkhand

Jharkhand is known for its rich forest resources, which support the sericulture of **Tussar Silk** and **Kuchai Silk**. These textiles represent a sustainable and traditional craft deeply rooted in tribal culture.

Tussar Silk

Tussar silk, also known as 'wild silk', is produced from silkworms that feed on trees like Sal and Arjun. It has a rich texture and natural gold sheen, making it popular for ethnic wear.

Weaving Technique: Hand-spun and hand-woven using indigenous methods; yarn is often dyed using natural colors.

Artisan Work: Tribal communities in villages like Bhagaiya and Chaibasa are key contributors to Tussar silk production.

GI Tag: Bhagalpur Tussar (extends to Jharkhand – GI Number: 147)

Kuchai Silk

Kuchai Silk is a special variety of Tussar silk produced in the Kuchai block of Jharkhand. It is completely indigenous and uses non-mulberry silkworms reared in natural environments.

Technique: Forest-based sericulture with eco-friendly processing methods and handcrafted weaving.

Craftsmanship: Emphasizes sustainability, community empowerment, and tribal participation in the textile value chain.

GI Tag: Kuchai Tussar Silk (Registered in 2008, GI Number: 103)
