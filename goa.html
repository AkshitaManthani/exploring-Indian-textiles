Textiles of Goa

Goa’s textile tradition is rich in its unique styles, with the "Kunbi Saree" being a prominent cultural attire. This saree reflects the vibrant Goan culture, combining earthy tones and intricate weaving techniques.

Kunbi Saree

The Kunbi saree is worn by the indigenous women of Goa and is made of handwoven cotton. Known for its simple yet striking designs, it is traditionally adorned with earthy tones and geometric patterns.

Weaving Technique: The saree is woven using traditional handloom techniques with cotton threads, creating geometric patterns with the use of natural dyes.

Craftsmanship: The saree’s creation involves skilled artisans working with the loom, incorporating traditional motifs that reflect the cultural heritage of the Goan people.

GI Tag: Kunbi Saree (Registered in 2012, GI Number: 214)
