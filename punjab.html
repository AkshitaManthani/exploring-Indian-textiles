Textiles of Punjab

Punjab’s textile legacy is deeply rooted in its rural traditions, beautifully expressed through the warm weaves of Khes and the vibrant embroidery of Phulkari.

Khes

Khes is a thick cotton fabric traditionally handwoven in Punjab, often used as a wrap or bed cover. Its simple checkered or striped patterns reflect rustic charm.

Weaving Technique: Handloom woven using cotton threads in bold check patterns. Often made on domestic looms.

Utility: Used as shawls, blankets, and floor coverings; known for comfort and warmth.

Craftsmanship: Woven mainly by women in rural households, maintaining generational weaving skills.

Phulkari

Phulkari, meaning "flower work," is the traditional embroidery of Punjab characterized by intricate floral patterns made using floss silk on handspun khaddar fabric.

Embroidery Style: Darn stitch on the wrong side of coarse cotton cloth, creating vivid floral designs on the front side.

Symbolism: Used in festivals, weddings, and rituals; holds cultural and emotional significance in Punjabi households.

GI Tag: Phulkari (Registered in 2011, GI Number: 172)
