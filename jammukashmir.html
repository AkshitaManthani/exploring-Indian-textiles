Textiles of Jammu & Kashmir

Jammu and Kashmir, with its breathtaking beauty, is also home to exquisite textiles like Raffal and the world-renowned Pashmina Shawls, both embodying luxury, warmth, and artistic finesse.

Raffal

Raffal is a warm woolen shawl, often woven with high-quality wool and adorned with traditional Kashmiri embroidery like sozni or aari. These shawls are popular for everyday elegance and comfort in cold climates.

Fabric: Made from sheep wool, softer and heavier than pashmina, ideal for winter wear.

Designs: Features elaborate needlework and traditional floral or paisley motifs.

Artistry: Embroidery adds value and uniqueness, often involving weeks of handcrafting.

Pashmina Shawls

Pashmina shawls are a symbol of timeless luxury, woven from the fine undercoat of the Changthangi goat. They are lightweight, warm, and treasured worldwide for their unmatched softness and delicate embroidery.

Material: Pure pashmina wool, sometimes blended with silk for sheen and durability.

Techniques: Entirely handcrafted—from wool collection, spinning, weaving, to embroidery.

GI Tag: Kashmir Pashmina (Registered in 2008, GI Number: 90)
